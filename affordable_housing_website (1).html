About the Issue

Narragansett’s coastal charm is undeniable—but for many locals, the cost of living has become unbearable. With rent averaging over $3,000/month, long-time residents are being pushed out by short-term vacation rentals and off-campus student demand.

Understanding the Root Causes

Short-term rentals, strict zoning laws, and investor-owned properties are major factors driving up housing costs. Narragansett's regulations favor single-family homes, limiting the development of affordable options.

[Insert Graphic: Short-Term Rentals & Zoning Impact]

Real Solutions

Other towns have taken action—and so can we. Zoning reform, rent control policies, and affordable housing incentives can help protect Narragansett’s future.

  • Limit short-term rentals per neighborhood
  • Allow duplexes and small multi-family homes
  • Offer tax incentives for affordable development
